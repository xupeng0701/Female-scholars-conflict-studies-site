Events

This page documents the workshops and discussions organised under the Female Scholars in Conflict Studies initiative.

Workshop Series

Project Launch

July 2025 | SOAS University of London

The launch event introduced the aims of the project and established its focus on methods, scholarly exchange, and community-building for researchers working on conflict and challenging fieldwork environments.

Doing Ethnography in Hostile Contexts: A Conversation with Professor Ching Kwan Lee on Her Decades of Fieldwork in China, Hong Kong, and Africa

February 2026 | SOAS University of London

This workshop examined ethnographic practice in difficult environments, with particular attention to long-term fieldwork, methodological reflection, and the challenges of conducting research across politically and socially complex settings.

Researching the Digital Illicit Economy: Methods, Ethics, and Access

15 March 2026 | Hosted with CIVAD, SOAS University of London

This workshop focused on methods for researching the digital illicit economy, with particular emphasis on access, ethics, and the practical challenges involved in studying scam industries.

Emotion, Care, and Common Concerns: A Relational Approach to Research in Contested Southeast Asian Borderlands

17 April 2026 | Hosted with the SOAS Centre for Southeast Asian Studies

This event explored relational approaches to research in contested borderlands, with particular emphasis on emotion, care, and shared methodological concerns in the study of Southeast Asia.
