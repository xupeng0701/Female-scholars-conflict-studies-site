Female Scholars in Conflict Studies

A researcher-led initiative dedicated to methodological reflection, community-building, and the sharing of fieldwork experiences among scholars working on conflict and other difficult research settings.

About the Project

Female Scholars in Conflict Studies is a researcher-led initiative supported by SOAS Research Culture funding. Launched in July 2025, the project was developed as a platform for methodological reflection, community-building, and the sharing of fieldwork experiences among scholars working on conflict and other difficult research settings.

While the initiative is grounded in questions of research method, its purpose extends beyond methodological discussion alone. It aims to create an intellectually supportive space in which scholars, particularly early-career researchers, can reflect on access, ethics, safety, positionality, and the emotional dimensions of research in hostile or politically sensitive environments.

The project also seeks to identify transferable skills and practical forms of knowledge that may help others navigating similar challenges. In this sense, it serves not only as a workshop series, but also as an evolving academic community shaped by mutual support, critical reflection, and shared experience.

Key Themes

  • Methodological reflection on fieldwork, access, ethics, and research practice in difficult settings.
  • Community-building among scholars working on conflict and politically sensitive contexts.
  • The sharing of transferable skills, practical strategies, and forms of mutual support for early-career researchers.
